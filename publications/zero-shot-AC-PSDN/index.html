Zero-shot Accent Conversion using Pseudo Siamese Disentanglement Network

Anonymous submission to INTERSPEECH 2023

1. Abstract

    The goal of accent conversion (AC) is to convert the accent of speech into the target accent while preserving the content and speaker identity. Previous methods rely on reference utterances in the inference phase or are unable to preserve the speaker timbre. In this paper, we propose a zero-shot accent conversion method, which is able to convert unseen speakers' utterances into a target accent. Pseudo Siamese Disentanglement Network (PSDN) is proposed to disentangle the accent information from the content representation and model the target accent. Besides, a speaker augmentation method is proposed to enhance the ability to retain speaker identity for unseen speakers. Experimental results show that the proposed system yields higher accentedness than the input, on two-way conversion including foreign-to-native and native-to-foreign. The naturalness of converted speech is also comparable to that of the input.
